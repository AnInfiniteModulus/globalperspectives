Jehovah’s Witnesses, despite the possible need for blood transfusions deny them as ‘the Bible forbids taking blood in to sustain the body.’ However, as long as health practices do not conflict with the Bible they are open to anything. Jehovah’s Witnesses also abstain from smoking and drugs as this strongly violates their beliefs, however, drinking is allowed as long as they do not get drunk. These practices end up benefiting Jehovah’s Witnesses in the long term.


When conducting our survey, we asked how they were treated for the common cold. In the west, people commonly drink chicken soup for a cold, as it keeps on hydrated and reduces inflammation. Most people in Asia/of Asian descent stated they did not see the point of eating chicken soup and said they would sleep it off instead. If that wasn’t enough, they would use western medicine.
